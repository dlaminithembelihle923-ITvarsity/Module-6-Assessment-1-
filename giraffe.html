GIRAFFE

The giraffe is an African artiodactyl mammal, the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. It is traditionally considered to be one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies.

Gestation period: Northern giraffe: 15 months Speed: 60 km/h (Maximum, Sprint) Class: Mammalia Genus: Giraffa; Brisson, 1762 Family: Giraffidae Height: 5 – 5,9 m (Adult), 4,3 – 5,2 m (Adult)

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

The reticulated giraffe lives in parts of Ethiopia, Somalia as well as North-east Kenya. Such subspecies of giraffe grazes around forests and savannas in these regions. Another example of a giraffe is the South African giraffe which lives in the Northern part of South Africa, Botswana, Zimbabwe as well as Mozambique.
